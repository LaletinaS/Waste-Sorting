How To Sort

1. Identify the type of waste

The first step is to determine what kind of waste you are dealing with. It could be organic, recyclable, or hazardous. This helps in directing it to the correct bin for proper disposal.

2. Organic Waste

Organic waste includes items that can decompose naturally. These items are usually biodegradable and should be composted.

Examples:

  • Food scraps (peels, vegetable leftovers)
  • Yard waste (grass clippings, leaves)
  • Plant material (flowers, branches)

3. Recyclable Waste

Recyclable waste includes materials that can be processed and reused in the production of new items. It should be sorted properly based on local recycling guidelines.

Examples:

  • Paper (newspapers, cardboard)
  • Plastics (bottles, containers)
  • Glass (jars, bottles)
  • Metals (cans, aluminum foil)

4. Hazardous Waste

Hazardous waste includes items that are harmful to the environment or human health and require special disposal methods.

Examples:

  • Batteries (AA, car batteries)
  • Electronics (computers, phones)
  • Chemicals (cleaning products, pesticides)
  • Paint and solvents
